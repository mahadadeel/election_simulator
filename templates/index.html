Can You Win This Parliamentary Election?

An Election Simulator Modelling Structural Inequality and the Rise of Political Extremism

You are the leader of the Liberal Party, an establishment, centre-left political party. After over decade of being out of power to the Conservative Party, you have finally won an election and now have a parliamentary majority.

You were elected on a simple mandate: Change.

However, the challenges your country faces are immense. A decade of austerity Economic policy to reduce budget deficit through public spending and benefits cuts, pay restraint, and outsourcing public utilities to private providers. has left public services crumbling. Wages fall far below the cost of living, housing is in short supply with high demand, the job market is stretched, and private companies owning public utilities like water, energy, and transport have squeezed consumers dry.

The Top 10% of the population own more Wealth than the Bottom 50%.

The people feel impoverished and are demand material change, but the many political and economic decisions leading to these circumstances are deeply systemically embedded. It is an overwhelming challenge to guide policy out of this inequality, but what you were elected for.

Can you govern your way to re-election in 20 turns, while fending off challenges
from the far-right Alternative Party and the progressive-left Green Party?

Every time you start this game, the election map is randomly generated.
Elections are run using a First Past The Post All seats are directly elected and single-choice. The winner only needs the most votes, even if they fall short of a majority. parliamentary system,
so results aren't proportional, and vote-splitting causes huge seat losses.
